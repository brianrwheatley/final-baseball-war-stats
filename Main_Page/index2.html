Predicting Major League Baseball Player Performance

using the WAR statistics to measure a baseball players productivity after injury

Project Overview

With 162 games in the regular Major League Baseball season and over 900 active players in the league, there are bound to be many injuries throughout 6 months of game play. Even the best of players will miss a few games to small injuries. Injuries ranging from small strains to UCL (Ulnar Collateral Ligament) tears and concussions that may take a player out for 5 games or for the rest of the season. However, if a player does come back to play in more games that season, how productive are they?

To answer this question, a historical dataset of WAR (Wins Against Replacement) and historical injury data for all players for the 2010 through 2021 seasons is used in a machine learning model.
